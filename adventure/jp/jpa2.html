The Mongolian Invasion

Japan

Reluctantly, you command your samurai to pray to Susanoo.


The soldiers and even civilians are now in much better spirits, believing in their hearts that they will be saved. But you know that now, without proper fortifications, there will be no way to stop the Mongols without a miracle. You worry and though you decide to join the samurai in prayer, your spirits stay filled with dread.

The next day you wake up to see the Mongols ready to attack again. You prepare your troops for battle, each one believing that they will win except you. As the Mongols land, the sky turns dark and huge clouds begin to roll in. A light rain begins to fall as your soldiers bravely fight and with growing confidence and strength in the air they push back the Mongols to their ships. But while the Mongols are regrouping, a massive storm the likes of which you have never seen comes roaring in. You soldiers start to cheer in celebration, but you are still fear the worst. Then you see that the storm is pushing the Mongol fleets out into the ocean, and on the decks the soldiers are struggling to control the motley ships. As the Mongol fleets disappear into the wind and rain, you join in the celebration.

A few years later, you receive a letter from the Kublai Khan himself. In the letter, the Khan recognizes you as the supreme leader of Japan, and says that he will not risk any more men attempting to conquer your islands.

You have beaten the Mongols once and for all, and Japan will not be threatened with a military invasion for hundreds of years to come.
