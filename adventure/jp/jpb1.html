The Mongolian Invasion

Japan

You decide to attack the Mongols where they landed.


Every one of your soldiers is excited and ready to fight. They want the invaders off of the island almost as much as you. After a few days of marching you arrive at Fukuoka. Your scout underestimated. There are 150,000 Mongol troops, a force you doubt you could combat. You decide to besiege Fukuoka and order a your soldiers to encircle the city in preparation. You know that the Mongols have no where to go and that they will run out of supplies soon. The Mongols, knowing the fact as well, counterattack in an attempt to break through your forces. The Japanese army is overwhelmed and quickly you order a retreat, but it comes far too late.

Your army is crushed. The Mongols take you prisoner and send you back to China to be Kublai Khan’s personal toilet warmer.
