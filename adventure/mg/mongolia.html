The Mongolian Invasion

Mongolia

You are Shinedu, a Mongol general.

Your glorious leader Kublai Khan wants to crush the imbeciles that reside on the islands they call Japan. He has provided you with a large army and navy because you need to obliterate any Japanese opposition you face. You set sail from China with the hopes of crushing thousands of Japanese souls. At your command are 3,500 ships and 100,000 men, while a force of 900 ships with 40,000 men is quickly coming from Korea to augment your force. It is no matter that the ships that are and will be under your command are not powerful warships; their sole purpose is to transport the ground troops into Japan. From there on, the soldiers will invade the island and conquer it.

After a few short weeks on the seas, Japan finally comes into view. A wall on the beach protects the Japanese, and from the looks of it the wall is 4 or 5 meters tall. It appears sturdy, and you can see soldiers defending the wall from forts.

Do you wait for the second fleet to join your forces or are you confident in the ability and power of you own fleet enough to decide to strike now?
