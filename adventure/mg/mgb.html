The Mongolian Invasion

Mongolia

You lead your fleet to Japan, hoping a quick invasion will guarantee a quick victory.

You land in early June and quickly take out the walls and the coast forces and establish a beachhead on the northwestern coast of the island of Kyushu. You send word to the mighty Kublai Khan, asking for more troops and supplies for invading the interior and later invading the other islands. Meanwhile, you troops are reckless. You just won a major victory and with momentum on your side the Japanese are in retreat. Your soldiers want to press on to conquer the island now instead of waiting for more reinforcements, who should arrive within a year. If you can conquer Kyushu before the winter, you could safely rest during the colder months without much threat of Japanese counter attacks. However, although small rebellions and uprisings by the local populace could be easily put down, they will likely upset your morale and troops. But if you cannot capture all of Kyushu by the time winter arrives, you would have to deal with raids and attacks from the Japanese samurai, a very troublesome prospect. And, not knowing the size of the Japanese forces, you could lose all too many troops. You must make a quick decision, because if you want to take Kyushu, you have to move now.

What do you do? Do you build a fort to hold the beach or do you try and conquer Kyushu now?
