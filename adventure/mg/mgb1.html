The Mongolian Invasion

Mongolia

You decide to back up your victory by building a fort that should protect you from any Japanese reprisals.

You inform your troops of your plans and they want to celebrate first, seeing it as an easy break from the hardship of war. You let them celebrate, and even you are in high spirits. But meanwhile, while all the troops are drunk and celebrating, the Japanese samurai cross the Matsuura River in the night, planning to defeat you out before your fortifications can be built. The Japanese have superior numbers, but the Mongols are famous for winning against superior numbers. However, you reflect on the difficulties of winning a battle with a drunk army as you try to convince your men to fight rather than stay in their customary stupor. But your efforts are in vain; the Japanese tear through your line and kill all of your men except you.

They take you prisoner, and you become regent Hojo Tokimune’s personal footstool.
